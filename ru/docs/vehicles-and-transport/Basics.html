Basics

Vehicles have three main parameters, determining how they handle:

Acceleration

Acceleration shows maximum possible speed change per Advance action.

Controllable speed

Controllable speed determines the speed bracket out of which vehicle becomes hard to control.

Keeping vehicle speed outside this boundary gives driver -1 to every driving action roll. Driver gets an additional -1 for each additional quarter of controllable speed above or below the boundary.

Maximum speed

Maximum speed determines the maximum speed a vehicle can accelerate by itself, without any external forces in its natural environment (e.g. atmosphere for plane)

Driving actions​

Only one driving action can be performed per turn. After executing the driving action vehicle will perform movement instantly. If no driving moves are made during the driver's turn, the vehicle becomes uncontrolled.

Uncontrolled vehicle

While the vehicle is in an uncontrolled state it keeps moving in direction it had before control was lost. Perform an “interact” action that costs 3 AP to try to take control of the vehicle. Roll against driving skill. On success, the vehicle becomes controlled again.

Steer – 1 AP​

Turn your vehicle, changing direction it is heading.

Advance – 1 AP​

Change speed no more than acceleration. Decelerate or accelerate.

Control – Vaires​

Keeping vehicle under control maintaining its speed and course. Might take more than one AP depending on type of vehicle.

Emergency stop – 3 AP​

Perform a driving skill check. If successful, decelerate by Acceleration∗3Acceleration * 3Acceleration∗3, otherwise vehicle enters uncontrolled state.
