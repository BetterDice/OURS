Traits

Traits make character special. They are more than just numbers on your character sheet. Traits define who your character is.

In other words, traits are special properties of characters. Some of them modify difficulties of skill checks. Some reflect character's background. Some give your character personality.

Traits have cost in Trait Points (TP). Cost can be negative if trait makes your character weaker. You are given a certain amount of trait points at the start of the game (a good amount would be 30).

When creating your character you can "buy" as much traits as you want as long as you can afford respective TP cost of trait and this trait is allowed by your Game Master. You can also recieve traits during campaign to reflect on your achievements or failures.

All traits that were aquired during campaign do not affect Trait Points of your character. You do not gain nor lose any TP when Game Master gives you trait.

Trait points should also be given as an additional award for completing important part of the story. You can spend recieved trait points the same way you would at character creation.

Trait categories

There are lots of traits. There is natural need to categorize them. That's why each trait has a set of categories (tags).

Physical​

Physical traits affect your body - how it behaves, how it handles different situations, how it looks.

Mental​

Metal traits affect your mind.

Social​

Social traits affect your social status, your connections.

Exotic​

Exotic traits define something that normal human could not possibly naturally be. For that reason in realistic campagins exotic traits should be banned.

Meta​

Meta traits do not fall in any other category. They do something really special which can change the rules of game.

Combat​

Combat traits help you fight and survive in battles. Any trait can help in battle but combat traits are more focused on that.
